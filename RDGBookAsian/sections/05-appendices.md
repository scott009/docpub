# Appendices

## Appendix A: The Recovery Dharma Inquiries

The inquiries are questions for deep reflection on our recovery journey. They are meant to be explored with curiosity and honesty.

### First Truth Inquiries
1. How has addiction created suffering in my life?
2. What forms does suffering take in my experience?
3. How has my addiction affected others?

### Second Truth Inquiries
1. What am I craving or seeking through my addictive behavior?
2. What am I trying to avoid or escape from?
3. How do I relate to pleasure and pain?

### Third Truth Inquiries
1. What does freedom from addiction mean to me?
2. Have I experienced moments of peace in recovery?
3. What would my life look like without addiction?

### Fourth Truth Inquiries
1. How am I following the Eightfold Path?
2. Where do I struggle in my practice?
3. What supports my recovery?

## Appendix C: The Five Precepts in Recovery

1. **I undertake the training to refrain from harming living beings**
   - Including myself
   - Non-violence in thought, word, and deed

2. **I undertake the training to refrain from taking what is not given**
   - Honesty in all dealings
   - Respecting others' property and boundaries

3. **I undertake the training to refrain from sexual misconduct**
   - Respectful relationships
   - Avoiding harm through sexual behavior

4. **I undertake the training to refrain from false speech**
   - Truthfulness
   - Kind and helpful communication

5. **I undertake the training to refrain from intoxicants that cloud the mind**
   - Maintaining sobriety
   - Mental clarity

## Appendix F: Glossary of Terms

**Dharma** - The teachings of the Buddha; the truth; the path

**Sangha** - Community of practitioners

**Metta** - Loving-kindness

**Mindfulness** - Present-moment awareness

**Dukkha** - Suffering, dissatisfaction, stress

**Karma** - Action; cause and effect

**Precepts** - Ethical guidelines

**Jhana** - States of meditative absorption

**Equanimity** - Mental balance and composure

**Compassion** - The wish for others to be free from suffering

**Wisdom** - Clear understanding of the nature of reality

**Concentration** - Focused, stable attention

**Craving** - Intense desire or attachment

**Aversion** - Pushing away or avoiding

**Impermanence** - The truth that all things change
