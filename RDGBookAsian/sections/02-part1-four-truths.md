# Part I: The Four Truths of Recovery

## Overview
The Four Truths form the foundation of the Recovery Dharma approach. They are adapted from the Buddha's Four Noble Truths and applied specifically to addiction and recovery.

## The First Truth: Understanding Suffering
We acknowledge that addiction creates suffering in our lives and the lives of those around us.

### What is Suffering?
- Physical pain and illness
- Mental anguish and distress
- Emotional turmoil
- Broken relationships
- Lost opportunities

### The Nature of Addiction
- Craving and compulsion
- Loss of control
- Continued use despite negative consequences

## The Second Truth: The Causes of Suffering
We recognize that craving and aversion are the root causes of addiction and suffering.

### Understanding Craving
- The pursuit of pleasure
- The desire for escape
- Seeking to fill an inner emptiness

### Understanding Aversion
- Running from pain
- Avoiding difficult emotions
- Rejecting reality as it is

## The Third Truth: The End of Suffering
We understand that recovery is possible and that there is an end to the suffering of addiction.

### The Path to Freedom
- Liberation from craving
- Peace of mind
- Authentic happiness
- Renewed relationships

### What Recovery Looks Like
- Freedom from active addiction
- Healing of body and mind
- Restored connections
- A life of purpose and meaning

## The Fourth Truth: The Path to Recovery
We commit to following the Eightfold Path as a way to end addiction and suffering.

### Introduction to the Eightfold Path
The Eightfold Path provides practical guidance for living in recovery. It consists of eight interconnected practices that support our healing and growth.
