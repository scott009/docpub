# Part III: Developing a Practice

## Overview
This section provides practical guidance for establishing and maintaining a daily practice that supports long-term recovery.

## Meditation Practices

### Getting Started
- Finding a quiet space
- Choosing a regular time
- Starting with short sessions
- Being patient with yourself

### Basic Practices

#### Breath Awareness Meditation
- Sit comfortably
- Focus on the natural breath
- Notice when the mind wanders
- Gently return attention to the breath

#### Body Scan Meditation
- Systematic attention to body sensations
- Releasing tension
- Developing body awareness

#### Loving-Kindness Meditation (Metta)
- Cultivating goodwill toward self and others
- Phrases of well-wishing
- Expanding the circle of compassion

#### Mindfulness of Thoughts and Emotions
- Observing mental states
- Not identifying with thoughts
- Developing equanimity

## Working with Cravings

### Understanding Cravings
- Physical sensations
- Mental urges
- Emotional components

### The RAIN Practice
- **R**ecognize - Notice the craving
- **A**ccept - Allow it to be present
- **I**nvestigate - Explore with curiosity
- **N**on-identification - See it as a passing phenomenon

### Urge Surfing
- Riding the wave of craving
- Recognizing the peak and decline
- Building confidence in our ability to not react

## Building a Recovery Community

### Finding Sangha
- Attending Recovery Dharma meetings
- Connecting with like-minded individuals
- Building supportive relationships

### Engaging in Service
- Helping others strengthens our own recovery
- Sharing experience and wisdom
- Taking on meeting roles

### Working with a Mentor
- Finding guidance and support
- Accountability in practice
- Learning from experience

## Daily Practice Guidelines

### Morning Practice
- Meditation
- Setting intentions
- Reading inspirational material

### Throughout the Day
- Mindfulness breaks
- Awareness of thoughts and feelings
- Practicing the precepts

### Evening Practice
- Reflection on the day
- Meditation
- Gratitude practice

## Dealing with Difficult Emotions

### Common Challenges
- Anxiety and fear
- Depression and sadness
- Anger and resentment
- Shame and guilt

### Skillful Responses
- Mindful awareness
- Self-compassion
- Reaching out for support
- Using meditation practices

## Relapse Prevention

### Warning Signs
- Isolation
- Dishonesty
- Complacency
- Romanticizing past use

### Protective Factors
- Regular practice
- Community connection
- Working with mentors
- Honest self-reflection

### If Relapse Occurs
- Return to community immediately
- Examine what happened with curiosity, not judgment
- Recommit to practice
- Ask for support

## Long-Term Recovery

### Continued Growth
- Deepening meditation practice
- Studying Buddhist teachings
- Exploring new practices
- Sharing with others

### Integration
- Living the principles in all areas of life
- Finding balance
- Maintaining perspective
- Celebrating progress
